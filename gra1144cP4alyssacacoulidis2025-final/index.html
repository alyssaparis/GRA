The Parker Playhouse, located at 707 NE 8th Street in Fort Lauderdale, is a cherished cultural landmark in southern Florida. Established by Dr. Louis Parker, it first opened its doors on February 6, 1967, and has since become a cornerstone of the community. This neo-classical theater, managed by the Broward Center for the Performing Arts, hosts a variety of performances including concerts, comedy shows, theater productions, and dance events. With a seating capacity of 1,147, it offers an intimate setting with no obstructed views, making it a popular venue for both local and touring acts. The Parker Playhouse recently underwent significant renovations, reopening in September 2021 with upgraded facilities and preserved artwork, further solidifying its role in enriching the cultural landscape of Fort Lauderdale.
